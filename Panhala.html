PANHALA FORT
|Information :

Panhala is a quaint and scenic hill station situated at a height of 3200 feet above sea level and is about 20 km northwest of Kolhapur. The little hill town is credited for being the smallest city in the state of Maharashtra and measures about 7 km in circumference. Panhala offers a beautiful view of the picturesque mountains and valleys of the Sahyadri mountain ranges and it allows easy access to the Konkan region.


Panhala Fort, also known as Panhalgad, is one of the largest and most important forts in Maharashtra. Built between 1178 and 1209 CE by the Shilahara ruler Bhoja II, the fort was strategically important as it guarded the trade routes passing through the Sahyadri mountain range. The fort covers an area of about 7.25 km and has triple walls reinforced with bastions.


The fort has great historical significance as it was the center of several battles between the Marathas and the Mughals. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj stayed here for nearly 500 days, making it one of the few forts where he spent extended periods. The fort also witnessed the famous Battle of Pavan Khind where Baji Prabhu Deshpande sacrificed his life to help Shivaji escape safely.


Today, Panhala Fort is a popular tourist destination known for its architectural marvels, historical significance, and breathtaking views of the surrounding valley. The fort complex includes several monuments, temples, and water reservoirs that showcase the engineering brilliance of the Maratha era.

|History and local lore :

Panhala Fort has a rich history spanning over 800 years. After the Shilaharas, the fort came under the control of the Yadavas of Devagiri, and later the Bahmani Sultanate. In 1489, the fort was captured by the Adil Shahi dynasty of Bijapur who made significant additions to the fortifications. The fort gained prominence during the Maratha Empire when Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj captured it in 1659 after defeating the Adil Shahi general Afzal Khan.


One of the most famous incidents associated with the fort is the legendary escape of Shivaji Maharaj in 1660. When besieged by Siddi Johar's massive army, Shivaji escaped through a hidden route known as the 'Andhar Bavadi' (Dark Well) to Vishalgad fort, with his trusted commander Baji Prabhu Deshpande holding off the enemy at Pavan Khind (Windy Pass). Local legends speak of secret tunnels connecting Panhala to other forts in the region, though most remain undiscovered.

|Things to look out :

Panhala Fort offers numerous fascinating features that showcase Maratha military architecture and historical significance:


  • Andhar Bavadi: A three-storey underground structure with a winding staircase leading to a water reservoir, believed to be part of Shivaji's escape route.

  • Sajja Kothi: The balcony where Shivaji was supposedly held prisoner by Siddi Johar, offering panoramic views of the valley below.

  • Amberkhana: The granary complex with three buildings that could store 25,000 khandis (about 50,000 kg) of grain.

  • Teen Darwaza: The triple gate entrance with massive doors and spikes as defense against elephant attacks.

  • Rajdindi Bastion: The secret exit used by Shivaji during his famous escape to Vishalgad.

  • Kalavantinicha Mahal: A palace built during the Adil Shahi period with beautiful arches and carvings.
|Key facts :
  • Built between 1178-1209 CE by Shilahara ruler Bhoja II

  • Covers an area of about 7.25 square kilometers

  • Elevation of 3,200 feet (975 meters) above sea level

  • Has triple fortification walls with bastions

  • Contains 110 lookout posts and 7 gates

  • Site of the famous Battle of Pavan Khind in 1660

|Nearby places :

The area around Panhala Fort offers several interesting attractions:


  • Vishalgad Fort: About 50 km from Panhala, this is where Shivaji escaped to during the famous Pavan Khind battle.

  • Pavankhind Memorial: Site where Baji Prabhu Deshpande made his last stand during Shivaji's escape.

  • Jyotiba Temple: A famous hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Jyotiba, about 20 km from Panhala.

  • Rankala Lake: A scenic lake in Kolhapur, perfect for evening strolls and boat rides.

  • Mahalaxmi Temple: The famous Kolhapur temple dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi.

  • New Palace Museum: A beautiful palace in Kolhapur showcasing the history of the Bhosale dynasty.
