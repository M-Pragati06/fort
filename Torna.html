Torna Fort
|Information :

Torna Fort, also known as Prachanda Gad, is a historical hill fort situated in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. It holds a significant position in Maratha history as the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at the age of 16, marking the inception of the Maratha Empire. Standing tall at an elevation of 4,603 feet (1,403 meters) above sea level, Torna Fort is the highest hill fort in Pune district and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Sahyadri mountain ranges.


In 1646, Shivaji captured this fort at the age of sixteen, thus making it one of the first forts that would become one of the forts of the Maratha empire. Shivaji renamed the fort ' 'Prachandagad' ' as Torna, and constructed several monuments and towers within it.

|History :

This fort is believed to have been constructed by the Shiva Panth in the 13th century. A Menghai Devi temple, also referred to as the Tornaji temple, is situated near the entrance of the fort. Malik Ahmed, a Bahamani ruler, captured this fort during the reign of Alauddin Khilji during the late 1470s.

In the 18th century, the Mughal empire briefly gained control of this fort after the assassination of Shivaji's son Sambhaji raje. Aurangzeb, the then Mughal emperor, renamed this fort Futulgaib in recognition of the difficult defense the Mughals had to overcome to capture this fort. It was restored to the Maratha confederacy by the Treaty of Purandar.


| Torna Fort Famous Fort :
  • Historical Significance: Torna Fort holds a pivotal position in Maratha history as the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at the tender age of 16, marking the inception of the Maratha Empire. This strategic victory not only provided Shivaji Maharaj with a stronghold but also ignited the spirit of independence among the Marathas.

  • Architectural Prowess: Torna Fort showcases the ingenuity of its builders, boasting intricate fortifications, multiple gateways, bastions, water cisterns, and underground passages. The fort's three main gates – Khindki Darwaja, Ganesh Darwaja, and Kondi Darwaja – provide access to different levels of the fort. The fort's inner sanctum, known as the Balekilla, houses the remnants of Shivaji Maharaj's palace, including the Bhawani Mandap and a water tank.

  • Panoramic Views: Standing tall at an elevation of 4,603 feet (1,403 meters) above sea level, Torna Fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Sahyadri mountain ranges and the lush valleys below. The fort's vantage point provides a mesmerizing perspective of the surrounding landscape, especially during the monsoon season when the greenery is at its peak.

  • Trekking Destination: Torna Fort is a popular trekking destination, attracting adventure enthusiasts who seek challenging trails and rewarding views. The trek to the fort is moderately difficult, but the stunning vistas from the top make it a worthwhile endeavor.

  • Historical Monuments: The fort houses several historical monuments that add to its significance. These include the Menghai Devi temple, also known as the Tornaji temple, situated near the entrance of the fort, and the remnants of Shivaji Maharaj's palace within the Balekilla.

  • Budhla Machi: Torna Fort is home to a unique structure known as the Budhla Machi, a large cistern that resembles a giant vessel. This water storage facility played a crucial role in sustaining the fort's inhabitants during sieges.

  • Wildlife: The surrounding area of Torna Fort is home to diverse wildlife, including leopards, Indian rock pythons, and various bird species. Trekking enthusiasts may encounter these creatures during their expeditions.

|Trekking and Tourism:

    Torna Fort is a popular destination for trekkers and adventure enthusiasts, especially during the monsoon season (September to December) when the surrounding landscape is lush and vibrant. The trek to the fort is challenging but rewarding, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and the majestic Rajgad Fort, another iconic Maratha stronghold.

|Tips for Trekking Torna Fort: :
  • Wear sturdy shoes and comfortable clothing suitable for trekking.

  • Carry sufficient water and snacks to stay hydrated and energized..

  • Be mindful of the weather conditions, especially during the monsoon season.

  • Start your trek early morning to avoid the afternoon heat.

  • Respect the fort's historical significance and avoid littering or causing any damage.
