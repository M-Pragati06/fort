SINDHUDURG FORT
|Information :

Sindhudurg Fort is a historical sea fort located on an island off the coast of Maharashtra in the Arabian Sea. Built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1664, the fort lies near the Malvan town in the Sindhudurg district. The fort was built to counter the rising influence of foreign colonial powers and to strengthen the Maratha dominance along the Konkan coast.


Spread over 48 acres with a 3 km long rampart, the fort walls are 12 feet thick and 30 feet high, featuring 42 bastions. The fort was constructed using locally available laterite stones and lead, with approximately 4,000 workers laboring daily for three years to complete it. What makes Sindhudurg unique is that it was built in the sea, about a kilometer from the shore, making it one of the most formidable marine forts in India.


The fort has several freshwater ponds that never dry up, a remarkable engineering feat considering it's surrounded by seawater. The main entrance is concealed in such a way that no one can locate it from outside. The fort also houses a temple dedicated to Shivaji Maharaj, where his footprints and handprints are preserved in lime.


Today, Sindhudurg Fort stands as a proud testament to Maratha naval power and architectural brilliance. Visitors can reach the fort by boats available from Malvan jetty, and the fort remains one of Maharashtra's most popular tourist attractions, offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea.

|History and local lore :

The construction of Sindhudurg Fort was personally supervised by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1664. The name 'Sindhudurg' means 'Fort in the Sea' (Sindhu = Sea, Durg = Fort). The fort was strategically built on Kurte Island, selected for its natural protection and vantage point overlooking the Arabian Sea. The construction employed Portuguese engineers and local craftsmen who used an ingenious mixture of lead, jaggery and lime as mortar.


After Shivaji Maharaj's death, the fort changed hands several times between the Marathas, Portuguese and British. In 1765, it was captured by the British East India Company. The fort was known for its impregnability - it was never conquered by naval attack. Local legends speak of secret underwater tunnels connecting to the mainland, though none have been discovered. The fort also has a sacred Banyan tree that is said to be as old as the fort itself, with its branches spreading over half an acre.

|Things to look out :

Sindhudurg Fort offers numerous fascinating features that showcase Maratha military architecture and engineering brilliance:


  • Main Entrance: The fort's main gate is ingeniously concealed and can only be approached by a narrow zigzag passage. The entrance has carvings of a tiger, elephant and two peacocks.

  • Shivaji's Temple: The only temple dedicated to Shivaji Maharaj, containing his hand and footprints cast in lime. The temple is built in typical Hemadpanthi architectural style.

  • Freshwater Ponds: The fort has three freshwater ponds - Dudh Talav, Dahikhana Talav and Papachi Talav - that never dry up despite being surrounded by seawater.

  • Bastions and Cannons: The 42 bastions house several old cannons, some still in good condition. The most famous is the 'Dhakka Bhairoba' cannon.

  • Ancient Banyan Tree: A massive banyan tree covering about half an acre, believed to be planted during the fort's construction.

  • Secret Escape Routes: The fort reportedly has several hidden escape routes, though most remain undiscovered.
|Key facts :
  • Built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1664

  • Construction took 3 years with 4,000 workers laboring daily

  • Covers 48 acres with 3 km long rampart walls

  • Walls are 12 feet thick and 30 feet high with 42 bastions

  • Built using laterite stones and special mortar of lead, jaggery and lime

  • Houses the only temple dedicated to Shivaji Maharaj

|Nearby places :

The area around Sindhudurg Fort offers several interesting attractions:


  • Malvan Beach: A beautiful sandy beach known for its clean waters and water sports activities.

  • Tarkarli Beach: Famous for its clear waters, water sports and dolphin spotting tours.

  • Deobagh Beach: A secluded beach perfect for those seeking peace and tranquility.

  • Rock Garden: An artistic garden created from laterite rocks with beautiful sculptures.

  • Nivati Fort: Another sea fort built by Shivaji Maharaj, located about 35 km from Sindhudurg.

  • Vengurla: A beautiful coastal town known for its beaches, lighthouse and cashew plantations.
