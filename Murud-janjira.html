MURUD-JANJIRA FORT
|Information :

Janjira is considered one of the strongest coastal forts in India. The fort is approached by sailboats from Rajapuri jetty. The main gate of the fort faces Rajapuri on the shore and can be seen only when one is about 40 feet (12 m) away from it. It has a small postern gate towards the open sea for escape.


Murud-Janjira is an impregnable fort in Raigad district. Its real name is Jajire Mehrub, Janjira means island surrounded by water and Mehrub means crescent. It is surrounded by the Arabian Sea. To the west of Raigad district lies the Arabian Sea.

|History :

Murud Janjira fort is one of the best forts built by Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in Kokan. This fort is located in the sea. It is accessible through Murud beach. This is fort is 2.30/3 hrs drive away from Mumbai.Murud-Janjira Fort is situated on an oval-shaped rock off the Arabian Sea coast near the port city of Murud, 165 km (103 mi) south of Mumbai. Janjira is considered one of the strongest coastal forts in India. The fort is approached by sailboats from Rajapuri jetty.


In the year 1736, Siddis of Murud-Janjira set out in a battle with the forces of Maratha Peshwa Baji Rao. On 19 April 1736, Maratha warrior Chimaji Appa attacked the gathering forces in the encampments of the Siddis near Rewas. When the confrontation ended, 1,500 Siddis, including their leader Siddi Sat, were killed.Padmadurg was one of the sea forts built by Maratha King Shivaji in 1676 in order to control the naval activities in the Arabian sea. It is located in the northwest direction of the Janjira fort at a distance of about 4 km.


|Major Attractions :
  • Entrance Gateway: The main entrance to the fort is a grand gateway with intricate carvings. It is flanked by two imposing watchtowers.

  • Darbar Hall: The Darbar Hall was the seat of power of the Siddi rulers. It is a spacious hall with a high ceiling and ornate pillars.

  • Mehtab Kothi: Mehtab Kothi was the residence of the Siddi rulers. It is a luxurious palace with a veranda overlooking the sea..

  • Masjid: The fort has a beautiful mosque with intricate carvings and calligraphy.

  • Water Tanks: The fort has several water tanks that were used to store rainwater. These tanks ensured that the fort could withstand long sieges.

|Nearby Attractions: :
    • Murud Beach: Murud Beach is a beautiful stretch of white sand with clear blue waters. It is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

    • Durga Devi Temple: : Durga Devi Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the Goddess Durga. It is located on a hilltop overlooking the village of Murud.

    • Padmadurg Fort: Padmadurg Fort is a smaller fort located near Murud-Janjira. It is believed to have been built in the 13th century.

      • Murud-Janjira is a fascinating historical site that offers a glimpse into the rich history of Maharashtra. With its stunning architecture, dramatic setting, and intriguing tales of the past, Murud-Janjira is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Indian history and culture.

|Special Things about Murud Janjira: :

The most special thing about Murud Janjira is that it is an impregnable fort that has never been conquered. It is located on an island off the coast of Maharashtra, India, and was built in the 15th century by the Siddi dynasty. The fort is made up of several layers of walls and towers, and it is surrounded by a moat. It has also been featured in several Bollywood movies.
