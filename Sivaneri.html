SHIVNERI FORT
|Information :

Shivneri Fort is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. Located near Junnar in Pune district of Maharashtra, this 17th-century hill fort has great historical significance. The fort stands at an elevation of 3,500 feet above sea level and is known for its unique triangular shape and strong fortifications.


The fort was built during the reign of the Bahmani Sultanate and later came under the control of the Ahmadnagar Sultanate. Shivaji Maharaj was born here in 1630, and the fort contains several structures associated with his early life. The fort's design includes multiple gateways, bastions, and water reservoirs that showcase medieval military architecture.


Shivneri Fort is particularly famous for its seven gates, each with its own defensive mechanisms. The fort has two main water reservoirs - Ganga and Yamuna - which provided water throughout the year. The steep climb to the fort, while challenging, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.


Today, Shivneri Fort is a protected monument and a popular pilgrimage site for those who revere Shivaji Maharaj. The fort houses a temple dedicated to Goddess Shivai Devi, after whom Shivaji was named. The birthplace chamber of Shivaji Maharaj, known as 'Shivjanmasthan', is preserved within the fort premises.

|History and local lore :

Shivneri Fort has a history dating back to at least the 13th century. The fort gained prominence when it was chosen by Jijabai, Shivaji's mother, as a safe place to deliver her child during turbulent times. According to legend, Jijabai prayed to the local deity Shivai Devi for a son who would liberate the land from foreign rule, and her prayers were answered with the birth of Shivaji.


The fort remained strategically important throughout the Maratha period. After Shivaji's birth, his family moved to Pune, but Shivneri continued to be a key military outpost. The British captured the fort in 1818 after the Third Anglo-Maratha War. Local lore tells of hidden treasure and secret tunnels within the fort, though none have been officially discovered.

|Things to look out :

Shivneri Fort offers visitors a glimpse into Maratha history and medieval architecture. Here are the key highlights to explore:


  • Shivai Devi Temple: The temple dedicated to the fort's patron deity, after whom Shivaji was named. This is where Jijabai prayed for a son.

  • Shivjanmasthan: The birthplace chamber of Shivaji Maharaj, marked by a statue of infant Shivaji in his mother's arms.

  • Seven Gates: The fort has seven well-preserved gates - the Maha Darwaja, Ganesh Darwaja, and others - each with unique defensive features.

  • Water Reservoirs: The Ganga and Yamuna tanks that provided water to the fort throughout the year, showcasing ancient water management systems.

  • Bastions and Walls: The fort's impressive defensive structures offer panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Ambarkhana: The granaries that stored food supplies during sieges, demonstrating the fort's self-sufficiency.
|Key facts :
  • Built in the 13th century, with additions made in the 16th-17th centuries

  • Elevation: 3,500 feet (1,066 meters) above sea level

  • Birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (born February 19, 1630)

  • The fort has seven gates and multiple bastions

  • Contains two perennial water reservoirs - Ganga and Yamuna

  • Declared a protected monument by the Archaeological Survey of India

|Nearby places :

While visiting Shivneri Fort, consider exploring these nearby attractions:


  • Lenyadri Caves: About 15 km away, these Buddhist cave temples date back to the 1st-3rd century CE and feature beautiful carvings.

  • Junnar Town: The historic town at the base of the fort has several ancient temples and the famous Ganesh Lena cave temple.

  • Ozar: Home to the Vigneshwara Temple, one of the eight Ashtavinayak temples dedicated to Lord Ganesha.

  • Harishchandragad: A famous hill fort about 60 km away, known for its spectacular Konkan Kada cliff.
